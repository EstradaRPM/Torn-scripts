Quick intro on RW gear
 
The ranked war system was released in December 2021. It is a significant update that revolutionizes how factions fight each other. The main reward for winning (or losing) a ranked war is arm caches. When a cache is opened, it gives an advanced weapon or armor with boosted stats and one (or two) special bonus attached to it.
 
The community often abbreviates advanced weapon/armor as "RW (short for ranked war) weapon/armor". RW gear is tradable in auction house, item market and through private trade. This gives rise to a massive market dedicated to trading them, which is still going strong now. 
 
Do you need a RW weapon/armor?
 
Before spending money on any RW gear, please consider these:
* Do you have a maxed bank? - A maxed bank is a great passive income to support your xanax use and PI rental. 3 month bank has an insane ~80% yearly return on investment so why spend money on a depreciating asset when you can get a free 1.6b every year. 
* Are you out of happy jump range? - Money's better spent on funding happy jumps, at low battle stats each jump give you a massive percentage boost to your overall stats.
* Can you borrow from faction armory? - Most top and mid tier factions have a well-stocked RW armory. Maybe have a look at it and see if you can borrow a wep or two during war/chain. 
* Do you need to fight frequently? - If your faction is mostly terming wars or you're in a revive/crime focused faction, RW gear is redundant. 
 
BUT! I just want a cool loadout..! I can't wait! If this is what you want, go for it, game's for fun! In my opinion, RW gear isn't really practical before you reach 1b stat and ~10b networth. Full combat set, diamond bladed knife, armalite m-15a4 was the meta for years and it still works well now. If you want to save even more money, just buy a macana (~100k).
 
Where can I get a RW weapon/armor?
 
* Auction house - No.1 place to get a cheap deal.
* Cache gamblers/Private sellers - You can often find them in forums. Most gamblers sell pretty cheap. This is usually the only way to get top tier weps that are not posted in auction house. 
* Item market/Bazaar - Generally more expensive than auction house.
* Traders - Traders are everywhere! In forums, trade chat, item market, etc. There's even an entire section later on how to become one yourself.
* Opening caches - NO! This is just gambling. Most of the time you'll just get "bunker bucks" crap that leads to a massive loss. In addition, caches are overvalued cos gamblers are pushing the market price up. Cache opening simulator for lols. 
* Community events - Rarely they give away RW gear from perm fedded players as rewards. 
* Display cases - The owner may forget they have weps stashed there so it may be a good idea to hit them up for a deal. 
 
If you don't know what RW weapon/armor you should get, check this section about loadout recommendation first. 
 
Determining the value of a RW weapon/armor 
 
The billion dollar question that people always ask, truth is, it's tough. This is also how an experienced trader gets your money! You MUST do research on prices before buying any RW gear. 
 
How to research prices (weapons)?
 
1. Screen whether the weapon is worth bunker bucks only
2. Check item market listings of that specific weapon/bonus
3. Check item market listings of similar weapons with the same bonus
4. Check item market listings of the same weapon with similar bonus
5. Stalk auctions (if available)
6. Check forum listings and forum history
7. Check bazaar listings
8. Check past auction/sales records
9. Ask traders
 
What is "bunker bucks"? (Step 1)
 
You often hear traders talking about "BB" or "bunker bucks". It generally refers to the minimum price given to a trash RW weapon/armor. People who have unlocked Big Al's bunker (2500 points in point building) can swap junk RW gear to bunker bucks, which can be turned into caches. 
 
You can calculate the price of 1 bunker buck by dividing the price of the cheapest small arm cache listing by 20. Check small arm cache price here
 
E.g. Cheapest small arm cache is selling for 120m, 120m/20 = 6m/BB
 
Multiply the value below by the BB value. E.g. 6m x 4 for a yellow pistol = minimum 24m worth
 
If you ever wonder why that trash lorcin achilles pistol still sells for 22m in auction, this is why.

1. Check item market listings of that specific weapon/bonus
2. Check item market listings of similar weapons with the same bonus
3. Check item market listings of the same weapon with similar bonus
 
Item market is a decent reference on pricing, especially on popular yellow weapons. However, the price you see is usually quite a lot higher than auction/its "sell-able" value. There's a reason why the weps are still on market and not sold already, it's because their price is high. High Quality/High Bonus % Weapons
 
A high quality yellow weapon usually has 130%+ quality, a really high one 140%+. A high quality orange is 200%+ and a red is 300%+. If the weapon base is good (like armalite, dbk, kodachi, btmp9, sig 552, etc.), having high quality pushes up value significantly. I often excludes them when finding price references, some people pay crazy money when they see a high qual gun. High quality doesn't matter much in bad bases. 
 
Low quality (<100% for yellow, <160% for orange) has effect on prices too, but usually less significant. 
 
High Bonus % means the higher end of the "range" . Let's use warlord as example, with a yellow range of 15-19% (rarely 20%). Pricing doesn't change a ton between 15-17%, but at 18-19% price curves up a lot. E.g. if you're looking for a 19% warlord, only check the 19% listings, the 15-18% isn't really a good reference. Another example is empower/quicken, prices from 50 to 70% goes up pretty slow, ~80%+ it's a whole different world. It's fine to reference a 55% or 65% if you're looking for a 60. But not a 80%. 
 
Double Bonus Weapons
 
Double bonuses weapons are usually overvalued, I won't suggest buying one unless the weapon synergize very well with your builds. It is also more difficult to resell them. 
 
--> As you can see, all the examples above are based on yellow weapons. It is really difficult to estimate the value of a good orange/red, double bonuses, niche or high quality/high bonus % weapons. They have very few price references on market and are usually too unique to price well. It generally becomes a "worth what the buyer is willing to pay" situation. This is also why orange/red weps have crazy margins, as long as you find the right customer.

 9140866b-984a-45cb-8025-6e5c51d25475-2804919.png 

I'll go more and bid 80m. This time, I "outbidded" Dan. It turns out his bid is $70,000,000 (70,000,001-1)
 5341b290-24e9-4c44-8b2d-fcdb10c866e1-2804919.png 

Clicking the gavel again and I can see this message. If anyone wants the weapon, they need to bid more than 80m to "outbid" me. If they bid only 71m, 75m, etc, they won't be able to do so. 
 
The price you pay for the RW item will be equal to how much the second highest bidder is willing to pay + $1, any money left over will become cashier's check. In this case, if no one bids me up, I'll get 80,000,000-70,000,001=$9,999,999 in cashier's check + the weapon. If someone bids 75,000,000, I'll get 80,000,000-75,000,001=$4,999,999 in cashier's check + the weapon. 
 
Most bids come in the last 2 hours before an auction ends. Don't get surprised by the cheap bids in the second/third page in auction. 
 
If I get outbidded, the full 80m will become cashier's check. I need to withdraw the cashier's check within 24 hours, or it'll be transferred straight to my wallet, which leaves me vulnerable to mugs.  
 
The timer will reset to 1 minute every time if it receives a bid below the 1 minute mark. This prevents bid sniping. I do not recommend bidding last moment - more on this later. 
 
Bids cannot be cancelled. You cannot rebid a higher bid if no one outbids you.

Tips and Tricks to get better deals in auction
 
There are a few things you should look out for when outbidding people:

* Safe bids - Some people may ask their friends to bid up their weapons, you may end up getting a bad deal if you win the auction. Be very careful if you notice any of these: 1. Seller is a trader 2. Seller and highest bidder is in the same faction 3. Seller and highest bidder are married.
* Noobs/p2w players - Plenty of noobs or pay to win players are overpaying in auction. If the highest bidder is <365 days old, below level 30 or have a blue name, be careful when you're outbidding them. Sometimes they even bid higher than the listings on item market, don't ask me why.
* Faction leads/co-leads - Not the kind of people you want to entirely avoid outbidding, but usually they don't really care getting the best deal just so they can fill up the armory... so be careful.
 
Who you should outbid - 
 
* Top traders - If they think it's a good bid, it's usually a great deal for you! (Oh yes are you going to outbid me for that? Betrayal!)
 
So many people try to predict a time frame that auction is "slower" and stuff goes cheap. It's just a myth. I have never reliably predict when auction will go slow. I do notice these three phenomenon:
 
* Certain holidays - e.g. close to the start of halloweek, people spend their time attacking and dealing with lags instead of bidding 
* Most auctions end between 14 TCT to 02 TCT - It correlates to the higher torn activity at these time frame. This does not mean stuff will go cheaper or more expensive if you buy it at this time frame.
 
Should you bid last minute or bid early? Fighting bids last minute may lead to behavioral mistakes (you keep bidding more and more and end up overpaying) and risk auction mugs. But yes, you'll usually get a slightly better deal bidding last minute, but remember to always protect yourself from mugs and set a hard limit on your maximum bid before you start bidding. 
 
It takes the system 30s to 2 minutes for outbids to show up in events. So, if you're gonna rebid last minute, stay in the auction page and keep refreshing it. Don't rely on events and notifications to tell you whether you get outbidded. 
 
Protect yourself from auction mugs - You may get mugged when bidding in auction house, especially when bidding last minute. Always have a ghost trade aside and minimize the time you'll be holding any money in your wallet. One tab for auction, one tab for the ghost trade and one tab for items (for medding out). 
 
High value auctions are often watched by muggers. If you get outbid and do not intend to rebid, don't withdraw your cashier's check right away. 
 
Cashier's checks are automatically transferred to your wallet after 24 hours. Don't forget to withdraw it before the timer ends or you may get mugged.

Dune/Riot = Bunker Bucks
 
Dune/riot prices are usually correlated to bunker buck values, often not more than 25m than bb prices. It's quite easy to hunt them in auction at bb or very close to it, as long as you aren't demanding one with good quality and high bonus %. Excluding riot helmet because of its special role in assault sets.
 
Assaults
 
Assault armors are a lot more interesting, market's flooded with them but has no sign of getting close to bb except assault helmet sometimes. 
 
I'll say don't think about it and try to bid 10-20% below the current item market value on auction. 
 
Hunting high quality/bonus % pieces
 
NEVER BUY DIRECTLY FROM ITEM MARKET. The listing prices on market for high quality armors are unhinged. Usually 3 to 4 times compared to base values (price of the cheapest listing of that armor piece) even if it's just some 40-50% quality pieces
 
Try to buy them at no more than x2 (or x2.5-3 if the armor piece really has very good stats) compared to base values from auction. Or you can hunt for good armors in forums and get a nice deal privately. 
 
Orange/red armors
 
If you want a price reference, check the market price trend in the last 3 years. You'll notice most of the prices stay more or less the same for years, sometimes it's even higher than before, but RW gear is supposed to depreciate in value over time, not too sure what's happening here. In general... this is a very weird market, erratic price movements - My advice is, take your time and buy them from auction one by one. Alternatively, go to forums and check if there are any end users liquidating their sets, they can usually give you a better deal.

Buying from traders/item market
 
I don't suggest you to do so if you're looking for the best deal. Most traders have a pretty big mark-up on their products, you'll usually do better buying from auction. Of course, you may get tired waiting for what you want to show up in auction, or the wep is so niche you can only buy from a trader. 
 
Again, ALWAYS do research before buying any RW gear (refer to the previous sections). After that, message the seller and ask for a MINIMUM OF 15% discount from the market listing price. Why 15%? Market tax is 5% and mug loss is 10%. It's totally fine to ask for more, but don't go too far or it'll be lowballing, which is very offending. If the listing's on forums, also a minimum of 15% from forum listing price, most traders expect you to negotiate down at least 10% so 15% will be a good starting point. 
 
Don't bother to buy from traders or sellers that don't even show their buyout offer, unless it's a good orange/red wep that's hard to pinpoint values. 
 
"obo" means "or best offer", this means the seller is inviting you to negotiate. "c/o" is the current highest offer. "b/o" is buyout. [B] means buying. [S] means selling.
 
Negotiation tactics
 
* Meet in the middle - Funny enough people are surprisingly receptive when you say this. E.g. Me: 200m for the ak warlord? Seller: 250m maybe? Me: Meet in the middle and 225? Seller: ok deal
* Round it off - e.g. (Seller counters with 205m) --> Can we round it off and do 200m?
* Give evidence based on similar weapons/armors on the market - Most traders are happy to at least match the price you see on market. You can also point out a similar or better wep is cheaper than the listing price as leverage. Say it in a way that's not rude tho. 
* Threaten to mug the seller - Looks rude but you can say something nicer like "is there a mug tax included in this listing?", exception is the seller is in a clothing store, although most clothing store traders are usually happy to give you the full 10% mug discount.
* The "frequent customer discount" - Applicable if you have bought stuff from the trader before. Or the seller is a factionmate or friend.
* The "bulk" discount - Applicable if you are trying to buy multiple weps/armors from the trader. 
* NEVER BUY FROM ITEM MARKET WITHOUT NEGOTIATION. ALWAYS NEGOTIATE!!!

How to safe bid?
 
Simply put, you find someone (like a friend) to bid on your listing. He will bid a price that you're satisfied it is sold for. If no one bids higher than that, the safe bidder will get the weapon and trade it back to you. Revisit the earlier parts on how to value RW gear and determine how much it should go on auction, minus 10-20%, this will be the safe bid. 
 
I suggest you to ask for a safe bid early, don't safe bid last moment, you may forget or your friends may not be online at that time. 
 
A trick to "bait" outbids is don't bid "whole number", if you want 500m for your wep, don't do a 500,000,000 safe bid, rather do something like 499,999,998 or 497,928,173. So anyone that bids 500,000,000 on it will outbid the safe bid.
 
If you have re-auctioned the same item for more than 2 times, it is time to lower the safe bid or wait a while before auctioning it again. Remember there's a 3% fee for auction sales.

Rate of Fire (RoF) and Magazine Size
 
This is usually an overlooked stat in a weapon. In battle, you want to avoid wasting turns to reload your guns and regulate your special ammo usage. That's why I often suggest a rifle or a melee weapon if you don't wanna bother with ammos. The Enfield SA-80 is an absolute beast when it comes to this. It has a 2-5 RoF and a magazine size of 30, meaning you can go 6-15 turns without reload. Even if an Armalite M-15A4 has better damage/accuracy than an Enfield, it only has a 3-5 RoF and magazine size of 15, meaning you can go only 3-5 turns without reload. 
 
This is also why secondary weapons are trash and usually seen it as a support weapon. Most pistols and SMGs don't have enough magazine to last you more than 3-4 turns. Heavy arms are even worse, that RPG launcher has 1-1 RoF and magazine size of 1.... this means you need to reload every second turn, Who cares if a minigun can load up 200 ammo when you're burning 20-30 special ammo each hit (great for merits tho). Shotgun has great damage but rarely last over 3-4 turns too. 
 
You generally want to look for guns with low rate of fire and big magazine size. In addition, I always recommend your first mod purchase to be the recoil pad - It's literally a 25% conserve in disguise.

Writing a forum post 
 
You should have a forum post to gain visibility on your stuff. 
 
You don't need to write excessive details or add in your sale pitches. Just show the stats and bonus and it'll speak for itself. 
 
Post title, keep it simple. Can be something like. Selling [certain weapon], Selling warlords, xxx's RW shop, Cheap RW weapons etc
 
ALWAYS include a buyout price, don't tell people to send your offers or make bids etc, this just makes things complicated. "Buyout price" means if the buyer offers this price you'll sell it immediately. 
 
An example of my post - Include the Weapon bonus, weapon base and your buyout price, (optional) damage/accuracy.
Make sure you always show you are willing to negotiate. the "obo" here means or best offer, inviting buyers to negotiate for a lower price.

Getting rid of "dead inventory"
 
If you have something that doesn't sell for a long time OR market price is higher than what you buy the weapon for. It may be time to cut loss. 
 
Usually I'll just cut loss if it doesn't sell after 1-2 months. RW gear is always depreciating, the longer you hold it, the value goes even lower. 
 
How to minimize loss? First you continue to drop prices. If you still don't get it sold, sell it in auction with a safe bid. 
 
Social Skills/Negotiation Skills
 
Always give a counter-offer even if the offer's a lowball. Don't ignore any DMs or messages. 
 
Always give a counter-offer even if the offer is good. Doesn't hurt to try for a bit more money :P, most buyers expect to buy it at a bit higher than the price they initially offer anyway. 
 
Always try to cut the deal right on the spot. People will just buy something else instead of waiting for you to make up your mind. 
 
You may find good buying opportunities outside of auction house. Befriend some cache gamblers! Don't lowball them (too much?) though.
 
Be nice to everyone and always avoid escalation.
 
Most RW traders are quite friendly but sometimes territorial. To be fair, you are directly competing with them! Make some trader friends and they may help you out with sales and give you good advice. Trading is boring without friends. 
 
You may sometimes receive random messages from people selling their gear. The first thing you should always do is check if they have a "merchant" title or have won any auction in the past 1 month. If they do, they are probably trying to resell you auction stuff. Check auction sales history to confirm. Usually I will just call them out for it lol. If you wanna be nice just say no thank you. I do not recommend you to directly buy RWs from other players until you're more experienced with prices. Auction is good enough.
 
Common mistakes new traders always make (important)
 
* Offer to sell your stuff to other traders. Please, just don't. First it's a waste of time, you think traders are gonna offer above auction value? Second it's very offending. How would you feel if someone outbids you, wins the weapon, then DM you and tries to sell it to you at a markup?
* Mugging other traders or customers. Horrible for business. If you mug traders they will just bid you up for fun next time they see your name on auction. 
* Buying from other traders. Are you sure you can flip for more profits? 
* Post auction buys on red/orange weapon selling thread. People hate it a lot. The people who scroll that thread are mostly lowballers anyway so don't bother it.
* Excessive forum bumps - please refer to trading post guidelines
* Complain about getting outbidded by other traders or end users. - This is a free market! What do you expect?
* Invest on expensive RWs without the networth to do so. - Example, investing on a 3b wep with only 5b liquid. If you value the wep wrong and it ends up becoming a dead inventory because no one is willing to buy it higher... you'll lose a ton of money. You'll also get a lot of anxiety because a lot of money is concentrated on one single item. 
* Buy BB crap and expect to get a very good sale on it. - No, you can't really convince a noob to take the ninja claw backstab or cupid ruger you overpaid in auction. 
* Overbidding - Too little research on the true value of something. Then end up becoming dead inventory and a loss. 
* Make a mess in their forum post and advertisement - Keep it simple, usually, the weapon speaks for itself. Excessive description and sales pitch on what it does is just going to be annoying. 
* Trying to corner the market without a good plan - I am not against anyone trying to monopolise stuff. But traders nowadays react fast, the moment you have like 3-4 same items on market, everyone's gonna outbid your auction buys and undercut you hard. Make sure you have enough liquid and a good exit plan before you pull a monopoly. 
 
More on market manipulation 
 
Does it work? Absolutely. This is how the super top traders make most of their money anyway. Some of them even manage to build a meta around them. To be honest, traders manipulating stuff is actually good for the rw trading economy. The market would be dead so long ago if no one try to keep it under control. Probably all yellows are gonna be worth bb now!
 
Should you do it? Probably not. At least not before you understand the market well. You don't wanna hold a dozen similar RW gear and then end up getting stuck cos there's no good demand. Sometimes you may unconsciously manipulated the market. For example, you keep buying the same weapon type/bonus and end up hoarding a lot of them. It is important to diversify your inventory!

Final words
 
Always, always, always pace yourself when trading RWs. It's a tough market. You'll be mugged, undercut, lowballed, outbidded, yelled at, made enemies, etc, It isn't pleasant sometimes. If you feel burnt out, please consider to take a break, just forget about what's happening in auction and retrieve all your inventory from item market/bazaar. 
 
The most valuable thing trading takes from you is TIME. It's something you can never get it back. Is it worth investing your time in trading virtual weapons and accumulating pixel money? Are you forced to trade because ched nerfed so many income sources? 
 
We all play torn for fun. Not to make it a second job. If you ever find trading becomes not enjoyable, just leave the trading scene and come back (or never) when you feel up to it again.
