Determining the value of a RW weapon/armor 

 

The billion dollar question that people always ask, truth is, it's tough. This is also how an experienced trader gets your money! You MUST do research on prices before buying any RW gear. 

 

How to research prices (weapons)?

 

Screen whether the weapon is worth bunker bucks only
Check past auction/sales records
Check item market listings 
Check forum listings and forum history
Check bazaar listings
Ask big traders
 

What is "bunker bucks"? (Step 1)

 

You often hear traders talking about "BB" or "bunker bucks". It generally refers to the minimum price given to a trash RW weapon/armor. People who have unlocked Big Al's bunker (2500 points in point building) can swap junk RW gear to bunker bucks, which can be turned into caches. 

 

You can calculate the price of 1 bunker buck by dividing the price of the cheapest small arm cache listing by 20. Check small arm cache price here

 

E.g. Cheapest small arm cache is selling for 120m, 120m/20 = 6m/BB

 

Multiply the value below by the BB value. E.g. 6m x 4 for a yellow pistol = minimum 24m worth

 

If you ever wonder why that trash lorcin achilles pistol still sells for 22m in auction, this is why.

High Quality/High Bonus % Weapons

 

A high quality yellow weapon usually has 130%+ quality, a really high one 140%+. A high quality orange is 200%+ and a red is 300%+. If the weapon base is good (like armalite, dbk, kodachi, btmp9, sig 552, etc.), having high quality pushes up value significantly. I often excludes them when finding price references, some people pay crazy money when they see a high qual gun. High quality doesn't matter much in bad bases. 

 

Low quality (<100% for yellow, <160% for orange) has effect on prices too, but usually less significant. 

 

High Bonus % means the higher end of the "range" . Let's use warlord as example, with a yellow range of 15-19% (rarely 20%). Pricing doesn't change a ton between 15-17%, but at 18-19% price curves up a lot. E.g. if you're looking for a 19% warlord, only check the 19% listings, the 15-18% isn't really a good reference. Another example is empower/quicken, prices from 50 to 70% goes up pretty slow, ~80%+ it's a whole different world. It's fine to reference a 55% or 65% if you're looking for a 60. But not a 80%. 

 

Double Bonus Weapons

 

Double bonuses weapons are usually overvalued, I won't suggest buying one unless the weapon synergize very well with your builds. It is also more difficult to resell them. 

 

It is really difficult to estimate the value of a good orange/red, double bonuses, niche or high quality/high bonus % weapons. They have very few price references on market and are usually too unique to price well. It generally becomes a "worth what the buyer is willing to pay" situation. This is also why orange/red weps have crazy margins, as long as you find the right customer.

How auction works

 

Number 1 place to buy RW gear. This is usually where traders get their stuff too. Quick rundown on how it works:

 

It is a lot different than real life auction. Let me give an example:

 

e15b9b31-0a0d-4e4f-9b99-781f1b16a4d0-2804919.png

 

First thing to note, the "$52,000,001" shown here is the bid of the second highest bidder (the second highest bidder bidded 52,000,000, the auction system will add 1 dollar, not something you should bother) , this is not Dan's (the high bidder) bid. Dan's bid can be a bit higher than 52,000,001, or a lot higher than that. You don't know how much he has bidded!

 

673de6fa-7f90-45da-8a89-aa21ae0e9903-2804919.png

Now, I am going to try and bid 53m. However, Dan's bid turns out to be higher! It is now at "$53,000,001", making me the second highest bidder of this weapon. However, it's still not enough to compete with his maximum bid. 

58fa7c39-d71d-41f2-8716-b6e65e0b0716-2804919.png

I'll go higher and bid 60m. Still not enough!

9140866b-984a-45cb-8025-6e5c51d25475-2804919.png

I'll go more and bid 80m. This time, I "outbidded" Dan. It turns out his bid is $70,000,000 (70,000,001-1)

5341b290-24e9-4c44-8b2d-fcdb10c866e1-2804919.png

Clicking the gavel again and I can see this message. If anyone wants the weapon, they need to bid more than 80m to "outbid" me. If they bid only 71m, 75m, etc, they won't be able to do so. 

 

The price you pay for the RW item will be equal to how much the second highest bidder is willing to pay + $1, any money left over will become cashier's check. In this case, if no one bids me up, I'll get 80,000,000-70,000,001=$9,999,999 in cashier's check + the weapon. If someone bids 75,000,000, I'll get 80,000,000-75,000,001=$4,999,999 in cashier's check + the weapon. 

 

Most bids come in the last 2 hours before an auction ends. Don't get surprised by the cheap bids in the second/third page in auction. 

 

If I get outbidded, the full 80m will become cashier's check. I need to withdraw the cashier's check within 24 hours, or it'll be transferred straight to my wallet, which leaves me vulnerable to mugs.  

 

The timer will reset to 1 minute every time if it receives a bid below the 1 minute mark. This prevents bid sniping. I do not recommend bidding last moment - more on this later. 

 

Bids cannot be cancelled. You cannot rebid a higher bid if no one outbids you. 

 

Tips and Tricks to get better deals in auction

 

Safe bids - Some people may ask their friends to bid up their weapons, you may end up getting a bad deal if you win the auction. Be very careful if you notice any of these: 1. Seller is a trader 2. Seller and highest bidder is in the same faction 3. Seller and highest bidder are married.
Noobs/p2w players - Plenty of noobs or pay to win players are overpaying in auction. If the highest bidder is <365 days old, below level 30 or have a blue name, be careful when you're outbidding them. Sometimes they even bid higher than the listings on item market, don't ask me why.
Faction leads/co-leads - Not the kind of people you want to entirely avoid outbidding, but usually they don't really care getting the best deal just so they can fill up the armory... so be careful.
 

Who you should outbid - 

 

Top traders - If they think it's a good bid, it's usually a great deal for you! (Oh yes are you going to outbid me for that? Betrayal!)
 

So many people try to predict a time frame that auction is "slower" and stuff goes cheap. It's just a myth. I have never reliably predict when auction will go slow. I do notice a couple trends though:

 

Certain holidays - e.g. close to the start of halloweek, people spend their time attacking and dealing with lags instead of bidding 
Most auctions end between 14 TCT to 02 TCT - It correlates to the higher torn activity at these time frame. This does not mean stuff will go cheaper or more expensive if you buy it at this time frame.
 

Should you bid last minute or bid early? Fighting bids last minute may lead to behavioral mistakes (you keep bidding more and more and end up overpaying) and risk auction mugs. But yes, you'll usually get a slightly better deal bidding last minute, remember to always protect yourself from mugs and set a hard limit on your maximum bid before you start bidding. 

 

It takes the system 30s to 2 minutes for outbids to show up in events. So, if you're gonna rebid last minute, stay in the auction page and keep refreshing it. Don't rely on events and notifications to tell you whether you get outbidded. 

 

Protect yourself from auction mugs - You may get mugged when bidding in auction house, especially when bidding last minute. Always have a ghost trade aside and minimize the time you'll be holding any money in your wallet. One tab for auction, one tab for the ghost trade and one tab for items (for medding out). 

 

High value auctions are often watched by muggers. If you get outbid and do not intend to rebid, don't withdraw your cashier's check right away. 

 

Cashier's checks are automatically transferred to your wallet after 24 hours. Don't forget to withdraw it before the timer ends or you may get mugged.

Is it dune or riot (excluding riot helmet)? - If yes, try to buy as close to bunker bucks as possible

Dune/Riot = Bunker Bucks

 

Dune/riot prices are usually correlated to bunker buck values, often not more than 25m than bb prices. It's quite easy to hunt them in auction at bb or very close to it, as long as you aren't demanding one with good quality and high bonus %. Excluding riot helmet because of its special role in assault sets.

 

Assaults

 

Assault armors are a lot more interesting, market's flooded with them but has no sign of getting close to bb except assault helmet sometimes. It is one of the best entry armor for wars, so demand is always fairly high. 

 

I'll say don't think about it and try to bid about what auction usually goes for according to the sales history you find from auction scripts. 

 

Hunting high quality/bonus % pieces

 

NEVER BUY DIRECTLY FROM ITEM MARKET. The listing prices on market for high quality armors are unhinged. Usually 3 to 4 times compared to base values (price of the cheapest listing of that armor piece) even if it's just some 40-50% quality pieces

 

Try to buy them at no more than x2 (or x2.5-3 if the armor piece really has very good stats) compared to base values from auction. Or you can hunt for good armors in forums and get a nice deal privately. 

 

Orange/red armors

 

If you want a price reference, check the market price trend in the last 3 years. You'll notice most of the prices stay more or less the same for years, sometimes it's even higher than before, but RW gear is supposed to depreciate in value over time, not too sure what's happening here. In general... this is a very weird market, erratic price movements - My advice is, take your time and buy them from auction one by one. Alternatively, go to forums and check if there are any end users liquidating their sets, they can usually give you a better deal.

Buying from traders/item market

 

I don't suggest you to do so if you're looking for the best deal. Most traders have a pretty big mark-up on their products, you'll usually do better buying from auction. Of course, you may get tired waiting for what you want to show up in auction, or the wep is so niche you can only buy from a trader. 

 

Again, ALWAYS do research before buying any RW gear (refer to the previous sections). After that, message the seller and ask for a MINIMUM OF 15% discount from the market listing price. Why 15%? Market tax is 5% and mug loss is 10%. It's totally fine to ask for more, but don't go too far or it'll be lowballing, which is very offending. If the listing's on forums, also a minimum of 15% from forum listing price, most traders expect you to negotiate down at least 10% so 15% will be a good starting point. 

 

Don't bother to buy from traders or sellers that don't even show their buyout offer, unless it's a good orange/red wep that's hard to pinpoint values. 

 

"obo" means "or best offer", this means the seller is inviting you to negotiate. "c/o" is the current highest offer. "b/o" is buyout. [B] means buying. [S] means selling.

 

Negotiation tactics

 

Meet in the middle - Funny enough people are surprisingly receptive when you say this. E.g. Me: 200m for the ak warlord? Seller: 250m maybe? Me: Meet in the middle and 225? Seller: ok deal
Round it off - e.g. (Seller counters with 205m) --> Can we round it off and do 200m?
Give evidence based on similar weapons/armors on the market - Most traders are happy to at least match the price you see on market. You can also point out a similar or better wep is cheaper than the listing price as leverage. Say it in a way that's not rude tho. 
Threaten to mug the seller - Looks rude but you can say something nicer like "is there a mug tax included in this listing?", exception is the seller is in a clothing store, although most clothing store traders are usually happy to give you the full 10% mug discount.
The "frequent customer discount" - Applicable if you have bought stuff from the trader before. Or the seller is a factionmate or friend.
The "bulk" discount - Applicable if you are trying to buy multiple weps/armors from the trader. 
NEVER BUY FROM ITEM MARKET WITHOUT NEGOTIATION. ALWAYS NEGOTIATE!!! 
 

I don't recommend you to do swaps or non-cash offer, it usually makes the trade complicated.

 

Please be nice to traders. Trading is rough and exhausting. Remember, the trader just wanna profit, and you wanna get your fighting gear. No point to harass anyone if the deal doesn't go your way. Just say thanks and move on if you don't like it. Please don't rush an answer or expect the trader to answer you immediately, they have a job outside torn too.

 

After you finish the trade, like and leave a comment on the trader's forum thread if they have one.

 

Small remarks on market manipulation/price fixing

 

This is something you should be cautious about - some parts of the RW market are heavily manipulated by certain traders. For weapons, you can see if it's manipulated by checking whether a single trader is holding 3 or more of the same weapon bonus/type + he has the cheapest listings among the other listings. Armors are usually manipulated anyway. To be honest, there really isn't much you can do. You can try to grab them cheap in auction but the manipulator will just bid you up (still it'll be cheaper than directly buying it from him!). I suggest you to try buy these weps through private trade, although the seller may still reference market price and refuse to give you a reasonable deal. Try to look for alternatives if you are willing to. 

 

Selling RW weapons/armors

 

Just a quick paragraph on how to sell your gear if you are not a trader. EXPECT LOSS, especially on stuff you have bought a long time ago. Please lower expectations on how much you'll get. DON'T SELL GOOD WEAPONS/ARMORS TO TRADERS! They usually offer below auction value, you can do better auctioning yourself. 

 

My recommendation is:


Bunker buck weapons (not armors) - Sell it to a trader that buys bunker buck weps, these shit aren't worth your time, alternatively, just do a 1 day auction. 
Dune or riot gear - 1 day auction, don't bother with safe bid. 
Yellow weapons/other armors - 1 day auction with a conservative safe bid 
Good orange/red weapons/armors - 3 days+ auction with a conservative safe bid, while advertising on forums and orange/red weapon selling thread
 

Forum etiquette - Strictly post in trading post, don't bump your post for more than once every hour, no more than 2 concurrent posts at the same time. Details here. In your post, you don't need to show off fancy battle logs or hard sell how good your wep is. Just say what you have, when the auction is gonna end and include a screenshot of the weapon/armor's stat. 

 

I do not recommend trade chat, which is usually for item traders. In my experience I rarely get deals even after spending hours advertising there, so don't bother it. Also don't recommend you to advertise in faction or company chat, it'll look spammy. Any discord #trade channel is fine.

 

Safe bid/Reserve bid is very important, there's always a small chance your gear will be sold far, far, far lower than normal market value on auction, rarely even below bunker price. 

 

How to safe bid?

 

Simply put, you find someone (like a friend) to bid on your listing. He will bid a price that you're satisfied it is sold for. If no one bids higher than that, the safe bidder will get the weapon and trade it back to you. Revisit the earlier parts on how to value RW gear and determine how much it should go on auction, minus 10-20%, this will be the safe bid. 

 

I suggest you to safe bid early, don't safe bid last moment, you may forget or your friends may not be online at that time. 

 

A trick to "bait" outbids is don't bid "whole number", if you want 500m for your wep, don't do a 500,000,000 safe bid, rather do something like 499,999,998 or 497,928,173. So anyone that bids 500,000,000 on it will outbid the safe bid.

 

If you have re-auctioned the same item for more than 2 times, it is time to lower the safe bid or wait a while before auctioning it again. Remember there's a 3% fee for auction sales.
