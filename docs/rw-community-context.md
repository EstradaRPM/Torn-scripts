# RW Auction Advisor — Community Context

Behavioral rules, trader mindset, auction red flags, negotiation tactics,
and common mistakes for RW gear trading.
Sourced from: King's RW Guide, RW Buyer's Math, 10yr old trading guide,
and 4yr old trading guide.

---

## 1. Core Trader Mindset

### The fundamental rule
Buy low, sell high. Sounds obvious — the hard part is the discipline to
execute it consistently under pressure.

### Buying is harder than selling
Selling is a waiting game. Buying is the skill. Put more effort into
researching before bidding than into advertising after. A bad buy creates
dead inventory; a patient buy creates profit.

### The ideal buy price
You are confident you could throw the item straight back to auction and
break even — or make a small profit. If you can't say that about a purchase,
you overpaid.

### Losing bids is healthy
Ideally you should lose most auction bids. Winning more than half means
you're overbidding. Patience is the competitive advantage.

### Stay liquid
Many small profits beat one large profit. Always take a reasonable offer
and move on. There are always more cheap items in auction. Don't let greed
turn a win into dead inventory.

### Don't panic sell
RW gear takes days, weeks, sometimes months to sell. That is normal. A slow
sale does not mean you overpaid. DO NOT drop prices or accept low offers
just because something hasn't moved in a week. For orange/red gear, 2–3
months is expected.

### Don't over-concentrate
Never put more than a comfortable fraction of your liquid into a single item.
Holding a 3b weapon with only 5b total liquid creates anxiety and leads to
panic selling at a loss.

## 2. Auction Red Flags (Safe Bids)

Some auction listings are set up with a "safe bidder" — a friend of the
seller who bids at the seller's acceptable floor price. If no one beats
that bid, the item trades back to the seller. Bidding into one of these
means you are competing against the seller's own minimum, not a real buyer.

### Signs of a safe bid

| Signal | Risk level |
|--------|-----------|
| Seller is a known trader | High |
| Seller and current high bidder are in the same faction | High |
| Seller and current high bidder are married in-game | High |
| High bidder is a noob / P2W player (under 365 days, below level 30, blue name) | Medium — they may just be overpaying |
| High bidder is a faction lead or co-lead | Medium — filling armory, less price-sensitive |

### Safe bid tricks sellers use
- Safe bid amount is set just below a round number (e.g. 497,928,173 instead
  of 500,000,000) to bait real buyers into outbidding with a round number
- Safe bid is placed early to anchor the price and signal demand

### Who you want to outbid
Top traders. If an experienced trader has already bid, it usually means the
item is a genuine deal at that price. Outbidding them is a good sign.

## 3. Auction Mug Risk

### Auction mug exposure window
When you are outbid, the full amount of your bid becomes a cashier's check.
You must withdraw it within 24 hours or it auto-deposits to your wallet —
where it is vulnerable to mugging.

### Protection tactics
- Always have a ghost trade or vault open in a second tab when bidding
- Minimize time holding money in wallet before and after bids
- If outbid and not rebidding, do not rush to withdraw the cashier's check
- High-value auctions are watched by muggers — be especially cautious

### Last-minute bidding risk
Bidding in the final minute increases mug exposure and leads to emotional
overbidding (you keep raising your max to "win"). Set a hard max before
you start bidding and never adjust it upward in the moment.

## 4. Script Mug Risk (Item Market)

### How script mugs work
Automated scripts (hosted in Discord) use the Torn API or WebSocket
connections to detect when a player sells an item on the market while
offline. They immediately attack and plunder the sale proceeds.

### Protection options

| Method | Mug protection | Trade-off |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| Include 15–20% mug tax in listing price | Covers loss but reduces competitiveness | Higher price = fewer sales |
| Self-hosp when offline (hire mercenary ~3–4m) | Prevents most mugs | Costs money, requires planning |
| Join a clothing store | ~75% mug protection, ~2–3% loss max | Requires company membership |
| Join a property broker | Potentially 0% mug loss, anonymous listing | Can't advertise, can't negotiate |

### Mug loss reference
- Script muggers with assassinate guns: typically 7–8% loss
- Manual mugs / red plunder weapons: up to 10–15%
- Repeated mugs while long offline: can exceed 15%

## 5. Buying from Traders vs Auction

### Always prefer auction
Traders mark up significantly. Auction is almost always cheaper for the
same item. Use traders only when:
- The item is so rare/niche it never appears in auction
- You are willing to pay a convenience premium for immediate acquisition

### Negotiating with traders
Always negotiate. Never buy at the listed price.

**Minimum discount to ask for:** 15% off listing price
- 5% covers market tax
- 10% covers typical mug loss
- This is the floor — asking for more is fine

**Tactics that work:**

| Tactic | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Meet in the middle | "Can we meet in the middle at 225m?" |
| Round it off | "Can we round that down to 200m?" |
| Use market evidence | "I see a similar one on market for X — can you match that?" |
| Mug tax signal | "Is a mug buffer included in this price?" |
| Frequent customer | "I've bought from you before — any discount?" |
| Bulk discount | "I'm looking at a few pieces — what's a bulk price?" |

**Never buy from item market without negotiating first.**
Always message the seller and ask for a discount before purchasing.

### Trader etiquette
- Always give a counter-offer even if the offer is a lowball; never ignore DMs
- Always give a counter-offer even if the offer is good — most buyers expect
  to pay slightly above their opening number anyway
- Try to close deals immediately — buyers move on quickly
- Be polite; traders remember rude players and will bid them up in future auctions

## 6. Selling RW Gear

### General principle
Expect some loss, especially on gear bought a long time ago. RW gear
depreciates over time. Do not sell good gear to other traders — they offer
below auction value. You can always do better auctioning it yourself.

### Recommended selling method by gear type

| Type | Method |
|------|--------|
| BB-only weapons | 1-day auction, no safe bid; or sell to a BB trader |
| Dune / Riot gear | 1-day auction, no safe bid needed |
| Yellow weapons / armors | 1-day auction with conservative safe bid |
| Good orange / red gear | 3+ day auction with safe bid; advertise on forums simultaneously |

### Safe bid best practices
- Ask a trusted friend to safe bid early — do not leave it to the last moment
- Safe bid amount: estimated sellable value minus 10–20%
- Use a non-round number (e.g. 497,928,173) to bait real buyers into
  outbidding with a clean round number
- If an item has gone unsold after 2+ re-auctions: lower the safe bid or
  pause and relist later
- Auction fee: 3% of final sale price — factor into your floor calculation

### Dead inventory
If an item hasn't sold after 1–2 months (3 months for orange/red): cut losses.
RW gear depreciates — holding longer only reduces final value. Drop the price
incrementally, then auction with a lower safe bid if still unsold.

## 7. Market Manipulation Awareness

### How it works
Some traders corner specific weapon/bonus combinations by holding 3+ of the
same item and controlling the cheapest market listings. This artificially
inflates perceived market value.

### How to detect it
- One trader holds 3 or more of the same weapon type + bonus
- Their listings are the cheapest on market by a significant margin
- The same seller name appears repeatedly in recent auction history for that item

### What to do
- Try to buy manipulated items in auction — the manipulator will bid you up,
  but auction prices are still usually below their inflated market listings
- Seek private trades for manipulated items, though sellers may still
  reference inflated market prices
- Diversify — find an alternative weapon/bonus combination instead

### Armor note
Armor market is considered perpetually manipulated by a small number of
wealthy traders. Treat all armor market prices as upper-bound references,
not fair-value references.

## 8. Forum and Advertising Etiquette

### Forum post structure
- Post title: simple and clear (e.g. "Selling Warlords", "xxx's RW Shop")
- Always include a buyout price — "mail offers" posts are ignored by serious buyers
- Show weapon/armor stats screenshot — the item speaks for itself
- Include "obo" (or best offer) to signal you are open to negotiation
- Keep it short; no excessive sales pitches

### Bump rules
- No more than one bump per hour
- No more than 2 concurrent forum posts at the same time

### After a sale
Ask the buyer to like and comment on your thread, and add you as a friend.
Reputation compounds over time.

### Advertising channels (ranked by effectiveness for RW gear)
1. Forum trading post — primary channel
2. Trade channels on Torn-related Discord servers
3. Private messaging faction leads and high-stat players directly
4. Private messaging recent auction buyers (avoid messaging traders)
5. Trade chat — low signal, high noise; not recommended for RW gear

## 9. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why it hurts |
|---------|-------------|
| Offering to sell gear to other traders | They will offer below auction value; it is also considered offensive |
| Mugging traders or customers | Traders remember — they will bid you up in every future auction |
| Buying from other traders to flip | Can you actually sell it for more? Rarely works out |
| Posting auction buys on the red/orange selling thread | Community hates it; the audience there are mostly lowballers anyway |
| Excessive forum bumps | Violates trading post rules; damages reputation |
| Complaining about being outbid | Free market — move on |
| Investing in expensive gear without the networth to support it | One bad buy can freeze your entire liquid; leads to panic selling |
| Buying BB-quality gear expecting a good sale | You cannot convince a noob to buy your ninja claw backstab |
| Overbidding from insufficient research | The most common cause of dead inventory and losses |
| Cluttered, oversold forum posts | Excessive description annoys buyers; stats sell the item, not words |
| Trying to corner the market without an exit plan | Other traders react fast — they will undercut and outbid you relentlessly |

## 10. Trader Reputation

Reputation is a compounding asset. Buyers return to traders they trust,
recommend them to factionmates, and are willing to pay fair prices without
lengthy negotiation.

### Building reputation
- Consistent pricing — buyers know what to expect
- Responsive messaging — always reply, even to lowballs
- Honest representation of item stats
- Polite, patient conduct even with difficult buyers
- Ask satisfied buyers to rate and comment on your forum thread

### Protecting reputation
- Never scam, never mug customers
- Be cautious about buy-mugging (hurts reputation even if technically permitted)
- Avoid hostile forum behavior — downvotes and forum enemies compound over time

### Working with other traders
- Most top traders are friendly but territorial — do not openly steal customers
  or advertise "I beat [trader X]'s prices"
- Teaming up with compatible traders can boost both parties — matching prices
  on a specific niche, referring customers, sharing intel on auction comps
- Trader relationships built on mutual respect are a genuine competitive advantage
